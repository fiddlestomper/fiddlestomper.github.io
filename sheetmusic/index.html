A note on levels

Some of the sheet music here is just as you’d expect. You have the melody, and sometimes a second voice (stämma), and/or chords.

Some tunes are noted down a bit differently. On some of them, you’ll find 4 different voices, referred to as “levels”. Since my belief is that music should be inclusive, I’ve started an experiment to make it easier for people to join in a jam session.

Some types of music (I’m looking at you, Irish folk!) are hard to join in to, and you need to have quite a good grip on the melody to be able to enjoy playing in a group.

American old time music, on the other hand, is the total opposite (in my opinion). If you know the base tones, and have a feeling for the rhythm, you’ll be able to join a more advanced jam. And as you learn the melody better and better, you can add more tones, and slowly “level up”. This is what the levels in the sheet music is all about.

Levelling up

The different voices, levels 1 through 4, are written so that they can be played together with minimal disharmony. So if you learn level 1, you’ll be able to join someone who is playing the proper melody, level 4. As you get more and more comfortable, you can add more and more tones by increasing the level, and before you know it, you’ll be jamming along with the pros like never before.

Just note that this is an experiment, and give it a try! Music is the most fun when you do it together with other people! So try it out and see how it goes, just remember to ask the other musicians at the jam if it’s ok!
