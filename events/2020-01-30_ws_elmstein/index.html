About the event

  • What? Workshop
  • When? 2020-01-30 - 2020-02-04
  • Where? Naturfreundehaus, Elmstein
  • How much? 140€ p.p.

Bright nights, mosquitoes and gnats, people swirling around the rotundas to the almost hypnotic 3/4 beat of a polska. The dance traditions of Sweden are very alive, and since a large number of talented Swedish artists are gaining attention internationally, many musicians have already made contact with Swedish dance music.

In this workshop we will take a closer look at the music from Svärdsjö-Enviken-Bingsjö. With help of the traditional tunes from the area we will explore some Swedish dances and their music: waltzes, schottis/reinländer, hambo, and (of course) the famous polska.

You will of course learn tunes, and we will put the emphasis on getting the rhythm right and make the music danceable. We’ll reach this by both listening to tunes played by Swedish musicians, and taking a look at the basic dance steps to get the groove into our bodies.

The workshop is open for all melody and rhythm instruments (as long as we have a well balanced melody to rhythm ratio). If you have an exotic instrument, just get in touch with me and we’ll see if it fits.

The target audience is musicians who can handle their instruments, and can learn tunes by ear.

The workshop will be held in English and/or German.
